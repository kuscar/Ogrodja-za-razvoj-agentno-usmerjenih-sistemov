## Nearby Attractions
### Landmarks & Activities
- **The Walk @ JBR (Jumeirah Beach Residence)** — 0.8 km walk. 1.7 km waterfront promenade with shops, restaurants, and a public beach. Perfect for sunrise walks.
- **Ain Dubai (World's Largest Ferris Wheel)** — 2.1 km. Iconic Bluewaters Island attraction. Book tickets online; evening rides recommended. Rating: ⭐ 4.7
- **Palm Jumeirah** — 3 km. The iconic palm-shaped island. Visit NAKHEEL Mall or the Atlantis Aquaventure waterpark.
- **Dubai Mall & Burj Khalifa** — 14 km (20 min by taxi). The world's largest mall adjacent to the world's tallest tower. Book Burj Khalifa "At the Top" tickets 2 days in advance online.
- **Museum of the Future** — 15 km. Stunning architectural marvel and interactive futurism museum. Must-book in advance.
- **Dubai Frame** — 18 km. Glass bridge connecting old and new Dubai; views of both Deira and Downtown.
- **Desert Safari** — Book through any hotel concierge; approx. AED 250/person. Evening departures from Marina area daily.

### Recommended Restaurants
- **Pier 7 (Level 7 Restaurant Row)** — Marina Walk. Seven restaurants across seven floors with 360° Marina views. Try Asia Asia (pan-Asian) or Atelier M (French-Mediterranean). Rating: ⭐ 4.5
- **Nammos Dubai** — Pearl Jumeirah. Greek-Mediterranean, glamorous beach club; lunch is spectacular. Rating: ⭐ 4.7
- **Ruya** — JW Marriott Marquis. Anatolian cuisine; best mezze in Dubai. Rating: ⭐ 4.8
- **Zuma Dubai** — DIFC (30 min). World-renowned Japanese izakaya; make reservations 2 weeks ahead. Rating: ⭐ 4.9
- **Operation: Falafel** — 5 min walk on Marina Walk. Best quick shawarma and falafel wraps in the area. Rating: ⭐ 4.6
- **Couqley** — JLT Cluster D. Authentic French brasserie; excellent brunch on Fridays. Rating: ⭐ 4.5

### Transport Tips
- Careem and Uber available 24/7; generally cheaper and safer than flagging taxis
- The Dubai Metro Red Line connects to Dubai Airport and Downtown
- Water taxis (Abra) run along Dubai Creek and JBR waterfront
- Avoid road travel 7:30–9:30 AM and 5:00–8:00 PM (heavy traffic on Sheikh Zayed Road)
- Noon to 4 PM in summer months: minimize outdoor exposure (extreme heat)

### Cultural Notes
- Friday is the Islamic day of rest; some shops open late (noon); weekend is Friday–Saturday
- Ramadan: reduced hours, no eating/drinking in public during daylight hours
- Dress conservatively when visiting malls, mosques, or souks
- Tipping 10–15% is appreciated but not mandatory
