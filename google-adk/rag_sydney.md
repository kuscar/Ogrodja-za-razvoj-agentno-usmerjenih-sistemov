## Nearby Attractions
### Waterfront & Landmarks
- **Darling Harbour Precinct** — 0.5 km walk. Sydney's premier entertainment waterfront. Walk along the promenade, visit the Chinese Garden of Friendship, or catch a sunset from the boardwalk.
- **Sydney Fish Market** — 0.8 km. Largest fish market in the Southern Hemisphere. Go early (from 7 AM) for the best freshness. Brilliant for a self-catered seafood breakfast. Rating: ⭐ 4.6
- **Barangaroo Reserve** — 1.2 km. Stunning foreshore park with sandstone headland walks and city skyline views. Free to enter.
- **Sydney Harbour Bridge** — 2.5 km. Walk the BridgeClimb or simply walk across for free via the pedestrian walkway. Views are breathtaking at sunset.
- **Sydney Opera House** — 3.1 km (easily walkable along the foreshore path). Book tours or performances at sydneyoperahouse.com.
- **Royal Botanic Garden** — 3.4 km. Free entry; walking to Mrs Macquarie's Chair for harbour panoramas.
- **Bondi Beach** — 8.5 km (25 min by bus/Uber). Australia's most famous beach; vibrant cafe scene; the Bondi-to-Coogee coastal walk is unmissable.
- **Taronga Zoo** — Ferry from Circular Quay (25 min). Animals native to Australia plus a spectacular harbour backdrop. Rating: ⭐ 4.8

### Recommended Restaurants
- **The Boathouse Blackwattle Bay** — 7 Ferry Lane, Glebe (1.2 km). Waterfront seafood with one of Sydney's best views; reserve ahead for weekends. Rating: ⭐ 4.7
- **Ester Restaurant** — 46 Meagher St, Chippendale (3 km). Modern Australian wood-fired cuisine; consistently rated one of Sydney's best. Rating: ⭐ 4.8
- **Rockpool Bar & Grill** — 66 Hunter St, CBD (3.5 km). Iconic Australian steakhouse, premium cuts, extensive wine list. Rating: ⭐ 4.7
- **Momofuku Seiobo** — The Star, 80 Pyrmont St (0.4 km walk!). World-class contemporary Australian with Asian influences (Michelin-starred equivalent); book 4+ weeks ahead. Rating: ⭐ 4.9
- **Encasa** — 423 Pitt St, CBD (3.5 km). Best tapas in Sydney; lively atmosphere; arrive early or queue. Rating: ⭐ 4.5
- **Kazbah** — 379 Darling St, Balmain (2.2 km by ferry). Moroccan-influenced; legendary Saturday/Sunday brunch (queue from 9 AM). Rating: ⭐ 4.6
- **Black Star Pastry** — Multiple locations; Newtown (4 km). Famous for their Strawberry Watermelon Cake. Rating: ⭐ 4.8

### Transport Tips
- Opal Card (pre-paid transit card) works on all trains, buses, ferries, and light rail. Purchase at 7-Eleven or online.
- Light Rail L1 connects to Central Station (for trains to airport) in 20 minutes
- Ferries from Darling Harbour to Circular Quay take 10 minutes and are scenic
- Uber/Didi/Ola are all available and competitive on pricing
- Avoid driving into the CBD during weekday rush hours (7:30–9:30 AM and 4:30–6:30 PM)
- Driving in Australia: keep to the LEFT side of the road

### Weather Notes
- Sydney has a temperate climate; summer (Dec–Feb) averages 26°C, winter (Jun–Aug) averages 13°C
- Sunscreen is essential year-round due to high UV index
- Heatwave alerts can occur Jan–Mar; stay hydrated
